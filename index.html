Self-supervised Audio Transformers over Log Mel-Spectrogram for Voice Conversion

Xiao Kang, Jichen Yang, Zhihua Huang, Hao Huang

School of Information Science and Engineering, Xinjiang University, Urumqi, China

School of Electronic and Information Engineering, South China University of Technology, Guangzhou, China

Abstract

In contrast to many state-of-the-art voice conversion (VC) systems that directly using log Mel-spectrogram (LMS) as the input, we propose a novel high-level acoustic feature for VC using a self-supervised audio transformers (SAT) based model trained on LMS, named SAT-LMS. Self-supervised training allows the SAT to learn high-level and context information from a large amount of unlabeled data. Thus, VC systems trained with SAT-LMS as input can benefit from knowledge extracted from unlabeled LMS. We conduct experiments on a VQ-VAE-based zero-shot VC to validate the proposed SAT-LMS. Experimental results show that the proposed SAT-LMS feature improves both the naturalness of converted speech and its similarity to a recorded speaker compared with LMS on the condition that we use enough training data and suitable model size for SAT.
